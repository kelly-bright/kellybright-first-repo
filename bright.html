THE THEORY OF DEMAND

Demand refers to the quantity of goods and services that consumers are willing to buy at a giving time over a giving period of time. A good definition of demand must include " quantity, price and time". Demand is not the same as need or want or desire for something. This is because the desire for something is not back up by the willingness and ability to pay for it. It only become effective demand when there is the ability to pay. The first law of demand and supply states that "more of a good is demanded at a lower price than at a lower price everything being equal"

DEMAND SCHEDULE

This refers to an imaginary tables which shows the various qualities of goods and services that a consumer will buy at a particular price over a given period of time. There are two types of demand schedule

  1. individual demand schedule
  2. market demand schedule.

FACTORS INFLUENCING DEMAND

  • changes in the disposable income of consumers
  • change in the price of related goods
  • changes in taste and fashion
  • government taxation policies and subsidies
  • seasonal changes
  • advertisment.
